Bhagavad Gita

About Bhagavad Gita

The Bhagavad Gita, nestled within the ancient Indian epic Mahabharata, is a profound dialogue between Lord Krishna and the warrior prince Arjuna. Set on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, this conversation unfolds as Arjuna grapples with moral quandaries and ethical dilemmas about participating in a war against his own kin.

In the Gita's 700 verses across 18 chapters, Krishna imparts wisdom encompassing multifaceted themes that transcend time and space.

At its core lies the concept of duty or dharma. Krishna urges Arjuna to fulfill his duty as a warrior, emphasizing the significance of righteous action. He unfolds the intricate web of duty, stretching beyond immediate responsibilities, woven into the cosmic fabric.
